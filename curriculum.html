Our Curriculum

A comprehensive journey through Qur’an, Arabic, and Islamic Studies, from foundation to mastery.

Departments and Subjects

The Himaayah curriculum combines classical Islamic learning with modern structure to nurture faith, intellect, and discipline.

Tawheed (Monotheism)

Understanding the foundation of faith, belief in Allah’s Oneness, His Names, and Attributes.

  • Categories of Tawheed
  • Shirk and its dangers
  • Conditions of Laa ilaa ha illallah

Fiqh (Jurisprudence)

A step-by-step guide to Islamic rulings through evidence, covering purification, prayer, fasting, zakah, and more.

  • Fiqh of worship
  • Fiqh of transactions
  • Marriage and family laws

Hadeeth

Study of the sayings and actions of the Prophet ﷺ with emphasis on understanding and application.

  • 40 Hadeeth of an-Nawawi
  • Riyad as-Saliheen
  • Principles of Hadeeth science

Qur’an Studies

Focused memorization, recitation, and understanding of the Qur’an guided by qualified teachers.

  • Tajweed mastery
  • Qur’an memorization
  • Tafseer and reflection

Arabic Language

Develop the language of the Qur’an, reading, writing, grammar, and conversation from beginner to advanced.

  • Arabic grammar (Nahw and Sarf)
  • Comprehension and expression
  • Balaghah (rhetoric)

Seerah (Prophetic Biography)

Tracing the life of the Prophet ﷺ, his mission, challenges, and leadership lessons for every believer.

  • Meccan and Madinan periods
  • Key battles and lessons
  • Prophetic manners and leadership

Adhkaar

Daily spiritual training through authentic adhkaar, short surahs, and beneficial supplications.

  • Morning and evening adhkaar
  • Selected adhkaar for memorization
  • Prophetic du‘aas

Character and Tarbiyah

Building upright character, discipline, and sincerity through guided mentorship and self-development lessons.

  • Purification of the soul
  • Time management and adab
  • Serving parents and society

Advanced Arabic (Nahw and Sarf)

Specialized for higher-level students mastering classical texts like Ajurumiyyah and Qatr an-Nada.

  • Ajurumiyyah and Qatr an-Nada
  • Advanced morphology
  • Arabic composition and syntax
